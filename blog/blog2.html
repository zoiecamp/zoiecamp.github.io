DevBlog #2: 02/09/2025 - 02/23/2025

Back

My focus over the last 2 weeks was adding juice to the game in the form of special particle effects for the buildings. I was assigned with creating effects for the wizard tower and the main tower. This was an open-ended and creative task, which was difficult for me because I work best when I have a clear guideline of what to implement and what it should look like. It also involved a lot of brainstorming and exploration in Unity’s particle system, which means a lot of the time I spent can’t necessarily be seen in the final result.

Total Time: 12h 15m

(45m) Finished Up Gear System

I spent 45 minutes at the beginning of the week finishing up the gear system from the previous 2 weeks, cleaning up the code and making sure the scene could get merged in without any conflicts. The main version of the game now officially includes a mechanism to build gear and equip it on adventurers.

(2h) Particle System Research & Exploration

I spent 2 hours researching & brainstorming ideas for the building particle effects. (5h 30m) Particle Effects Implementation

After some research and brainstorming, I spent the next hour and 45 minutes getting some experience with the particle system and implementing this very simple effect of sparkles swirling around the inside of the orb on the wizard tower.

I spent another hour and 45 minutes implementing this fire effect on the main tower.

Finally, I spent 2 hours creating this orb effect around the orb on the wizard tower.

My biggest challenge in implementing these things was the subjective nature of the task — not knowing exactly what effects to create or exactly what they should look like. My perfectionist tendencies definitely came out and I spent a lot of time making tiny tweaks to values in the particle system to try to create the “perfect” look. Despite that, I’m unsure of whether anybody else on the project will like or agree with these choices so all of this work may be scrapped, which is a tough fact to face.
