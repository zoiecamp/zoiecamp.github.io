DevBlog #4: 03/09/2025 - 03/23/2025

Back

During this 2-week sprint, I continued working on my task of creating an animated shader effect for the building ranges. Unfortunately, I continued to struggle significantly with this task and was unable to produce any good results. I’ve never worked with shaders or tilemaps before, and trying to combine them together was simply not working for me. I spent a lot more time reading articles and watching videos rather than actually working on implementation, and I’m feeling very discouraged by the fact that the amount of time I spent can’t be seen in any physical results.

Total Time: 12h

(1h 30m) Experimenting with Animated Tiles

I spent an hour and a half trying to build on my idea from last week of using animated tiles to represent the range of the building. I tried to draw new tiles to make it look nicer and this was the result:

Unfortunately, it doesn't look much better than what I had from last week, despite spending so much time on it. I decided to continue trying to get an actual shader working because I realized this animated tile idea is limited by my artistic abilities and by the fact that each tile can only animate individually, rather than having a cohesive effect across the whole area of the range.

(6h 30m) Experimenting with Meshes and Shaders

This is where I spent a lot of time reading and watching videos. What I wanted was a simple way to create a shader effect that was confined within the bounds of the tile map, but I was unable to find any helpful information. Shaders applied directly to the tile map only take effect on each tile individually. I was able to find people who had implemented something similar with a simple square shape, or with a predefined area for the tile map, but this range map is a very complex shape made of many hexagons, and is generated dynamically, changing as buildings are placed and the town expands. I was unable to find any sources from anyone who had done anything similar and I had literally no idea what to do.

I started by trying to convert the tile map into a single mesh, because it seemed like it would be much more simple to apply the kind of shader I wanted to a mesh object. This took a lot of time to figure out, but I was finally able to accomplish something and get this mesh object dynamically generated in the exact shape and area of the tile map:

Unfortunately, after sinking hours into this, I immediately realized that I have no way to render this mesh on top of the ground and below the trees and buildings, because it uses different properties than the sprites and has no sorting layer or order in layer option.

I moved on to see if I could somehow mask a sprite in the shape of the tile map. I placed this swirling effect sprite on the ground to experiment with. I thought this could look really cool if I could somehow clip it to the range and then maybe make it rotate or something.

I did a lot of searching for how to implement sprite masking, but everything I could find used only very simple circle and square shapes to mask their sprites. I was completely lost on how to make this work with this complex dynamically generated multi-hexagonal shape.

At this point, I had spent 6 hours and 30 minutes on this task and not even gotten close to having any concept of an idea of how to implement anything. I really wish that I had more to show for my time, and I'm feeling very discouraged by my lack of success or even progress. I think this kind of work with shaders and tile maps is just not my strong suit, and I'm hoping that next week I can move on to a different task and let somebody who has a better idea of what they're doing take over this one.
